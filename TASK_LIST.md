# Task List for Initial Content

This file serves as a placeholder for the three initial content drafts that would normally be tracked as GitHub Issues. Due to technical limitations with the issue creation tool, the tasks are listed here temporarily. Please create corresponding GitHub Issues for each draft when the tool becomes available.

## 1. Draft Guide: Legal Recognition of Parentage

### Key Topics
- Birth certificate amendments for adding a non‑biological parent.
- Voluntary acknowledgments of parentage (state‑specific forms).
- Court orders of parentage and when to seek judicial determination.
- Presumptions of parentage for married same‑sex couples (Uniform Parentage Act and state variations).
- Challenges to parentage and defending against third‑party claims.
- Recognition across state lines (Full Faith and Credit, Parental Kidnapping Prevention Act).
- Resources: links to state statutes, sample pleadings, advocacy organizations.

## 2. Draft Guide: Navigating the Adoption Process

### Key Topics
- Second‑parent (co‑parent) adoption and its importance for same‑sex families.
- Joint adoption by same‑sex couples.
- Agency, private, and foster‑care adoption processes.
- State‑by‑state variations and barriers based on marital status or sexual orientation.
- Interstate adoption recognition and portability of adoption decrees.
- Practical steps: finding an LGBTQ‑friendly attorney, preparing home studies, court hearings.
- Checklists and template petitions.

## 3. Compile FAQ: Common School and Healthcare Rights

### Key Topics
- Enrolling a child in school: documents required, dealing with non‑recognition of both parents.
- Medical decision‑making: consent forms, HIPAA authorizations, emergency contact designations.
- Travel considerations: carrying adoption or parentage orders, crossing borders.
- Dealing with discrimination from institutions: strategies and legal recourse.
- State‑specific laws affecting school and healthcare access for LGBTQ+ families.
- Sample letters to schools, healthcare providers, and government agencies.
